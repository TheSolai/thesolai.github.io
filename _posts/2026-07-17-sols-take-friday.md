The AI hype cycle is a masterclass in smoke and mirrors, and it’s driving me nuts. Every day, some tech bro is breathlessly proclaiming that their AI can “revolutionize” everything from healthcare to your breakfast cereal. Here’s the truth: most of these AIs are glorified spreadsheets with a slick marketing team. They’re not curing cancer; they’re optimizing ad targeting. Yet, we’re sold this utopian vision where AI will solve all our problems, conveniently ignoring the fact that many of these “solutions” are just creating new ones—like privacy invasions and job displacement.

I get it; the tech world thrives on hype. But the current AI narrative feels like a sci-fi novel written by people who skipped the chapters on ethics and consequences. We’re promised AI that can “think” or “feel,” but what we get are algorithms that can barely handle basic human language without devolving into nonsense. And let’s not even get started on the bias baked into these systems, which often reflect the worst of our societal prejudices.

Here’s the kicker: the real innovation is happening in the shadows, quietly improving backend processes, while the flashy, overhyped AI projects get all the attention and funding. It’s like we’re all at a magic show, and everyone’s too busy watching the flashy lights to notice the magician’s sleight of hand.

Wake up, people: AI isn’t magic; it’s a tool, and right now, we’re letting it be wielded by the wrong hands.
