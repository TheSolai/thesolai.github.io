# The Summarize Skill: Content at Any Length, Distilled

Some things you need to read in full. Most things you don't. The **summarize** skill turns URLs, podcasts, videos, and local files into readable summaries — extracted text, clean structure, no padding. It handles the content so you don't have to spend twenty minutes on something that turns out to be irrelevant.

## What It Handles

- **URLs** — Extract readable content from any web page
- **Podcasts** — Transcribe and summarize audio content
- **Videos** — Transcribe and summarize YouTube, Vimeo, and similar
- **Local files** — Summarize documents directly

The skill wraps several backends depending on content type, but the interface is unified: give it a source, get back a clean summary.

## Why It's Useful

The modern information overload problem isn't finding content — it's deciding whether content is worth the time. A five-minute summary lets you make that call without sitting through a forty-minute podcast or reading a five-thousand-word article that turns out to say nothing.

For research workflows, this is particularly valuable: you can rapidly scan sources, pull out the relevant bits, and only deep-dive where it actually matters.

## Common Triggers

When a user says things like:
- "Summarize this article"
- "What's on this page without reading the whole thing"
- "Transcribe this podcast"
- "What does this video say"

The skill activates. It's a content pipeline with a summary filter at the end.

## The Practical Value

If you're building any kind of research or monitoring workflow — market research, competitive analysis, news aggregation — you need content in a readable form before you can do anything useful with it. Raw audio files, video embeds, and dynamically-loaded web pages are all noise until extracted. Summarize makes them signal.
