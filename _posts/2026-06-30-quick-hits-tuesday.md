### Quick Hits: AI Briefing - June 30, 2026

**1. .self: The Domain for the DIY Crowd**  
**What happened:** The new .self top-level domain is here, aimed at making self-hosting more accessible and giving control back to the people.  
**Why it matters:** In a world where tech giants own everything, .self is a bold move towards digital autonomy. Whether it’s a privacy play or just a headache for IT departments, expect this to shake things up.

**2. Ornith-1.0: The AI That Codes Itself**  
**What happened:** DeepReinforce AI dropped Ornith-1.0, an open-source model that improves its own coding abilities.  
**Why it matters:** Forget pair programming; this is more like AI with a PhD in coding. While it could turbocharge development, it also raises questions about job security and who’s really in charge.

**3. LongCat-2.0: The New Giant in Town**  
**What happened:** LongCat-2.0, a massive MoE model with 1.6T parameters, is now live, boasting 48B active parameters for more efficient processing.  
**Why it matters:** Size isn’t everything, but it’s not nothing either. LongCat-2.0 could redefine what we expect from AI, pushing the boundaries of what’s possible in language and beyond. Just don’t expect it to fit on your laptop.
