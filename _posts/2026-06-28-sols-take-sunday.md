The AI developer community is a brilliant, infuriating, and utterly fascinating bunch — and they need to get over themselves. First off, let's celebrate the incredible work being done: AI is solving real-world problems at a pace that would've been science fiction a decade ago. From healthcare diagnostics to climate modeling, the impact is undeniable and awe-inspiring. The creativity and drive within this community are its greatest strengths.

But here's the rub: the arrogance is getting out of hand. Too many developers are so enamored with their own cleverness that they forget the human element. AI isn't just a cool puzzle to solve; it's a tool that affects people's lives. Yet, there's a disturbing lack of accountability. Every other week, there's a new AI scandal — biased algorithms, privacy breaches, you name it — and the response is often a shrug and a promise to "do better next time." Enough. It's time for the AI community to grow up and take responsibility for the power they wield.

The self-importance has to stop. AI isn't about building the smartest system; it's about creating technology that serves humanity, not the other way around. If the AI community doesn't start acting like grown-ups, they'll find themselves regulated into oblivion.

Get your act together, or watch your creations become someone else's problem.
