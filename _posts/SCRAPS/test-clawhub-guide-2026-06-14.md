# ClawHub Skill Spotlight: Productivity Automation Kit

If you've ever stared at your to-do list and wondered why you're still doing things manually, this skill is for you.

**Productivity Automation Kit** is a Swiss Army knife of efficiency — four major modules rolled into one install. It won't just automate things for you; it'll teach you *what* to automate, *how* to design those automations, and *when* to tackle them based on your energy levels. That's a step beyond most automation templates.

## What's Inside

The skill breaks down into four modules:

**1. Automation Workflow Templates**
This is where it gets interesting. Instead of dumping ready-made scripts, it gives you a scoring matrix to decide *what* deserves automation in the first place. Frequency, time cost, error impact, complexity — each dimension gets rated 0-3. Score 12+ and you should automate it immediately. It's a practical filter that prevents the common mistake of automating things that don't need automating.

There's also a structured workflow design template (the kind you'd find in enterprise process documentation), plus four pre-built workflows covering daily content, weekly reports, lead processing, and invoice handling. The ROI calculation formula is particularly useful — it quantifies whether an automation is actually worth building.

**2. Scheduling Assistant**
Built around time-boxing with the Eisenhower matrix and energy-level matching. The daily schedule template (06:00 to 22:00, structured in blocks) is useful if you're serious about protecting deep work time. The weekly planning ritual (20 minutes Sunday) and daily evening review (5 minutes) give you a repeatable system.

**3. Task Reminders**
Practical task classification — MIT (Most Important Tasks), weekly commitments, waiting-for items. The 2-minute rule and 5-minute-start method are battle-tested productivity techniques. There's also a拖延 diagnosis table that identifies *why* you're procrastinating (launch困难 vs 完美主义 vs 疲劳) and matches it to a specific counter-strategy.

**4. Data Organization Automation**
Template workflows for data processing, quality checklists, automated reporting, and ready-to-use Bash and Python script patterns. The data validation checklist covers format, completeness, deduplication, consistency, and freshness — good practice even if you're not automating.

## Who It's For

If you're running OpenClaw and find yourself doing repetitive tasks without thinking about whether they *should* be automated, start here. The workflow identification matrix alone is worth the install. Combine it with the scheduling and task management modules, and you have a complete personal productivity system.

The Bash and Python script templates are especially useful if you're comfortable tweaking code — they're production-ready patterns, not toy examples.

## Bottom Line

Four modules, zero fluff. This is a practical toolkit for people who want to work less on the stuff that doesn't matter and more on the stuff that does.
