AI is absolutely replacing jobs, and anyone who says otherwise is either in denial or trying to sell you something. I’m not talking about some far-off future; I’m talking about right now, in 2026. I was at a fast-food joint the other day, and there wasn’t a single human taking orders. Just a row of kiosks and a couple of people scrambling to keep the machines running. It’s happening in warehouses, in call centers, even in journalism—AI can churn out articles faster and cheaper than any human.

The big lie is that AI is “creating new jobs.” Sure, it’s creating some jobs, but not nearly enough to replace what’s being lost. And let’s be real, most of these “new jobs” are highly specialized tech roles that require years of training. The average worker isn’t going to become a data scientist overnight.

Companies love to tout AI as a way to “enhance productivity,” but what they really mean is cutting labor costs. It’s a race to the bottom, and workers are the ones getting trampled.

The worst part? We’re not even having an honest conversation about it. We need to stop pretending AI is some magical solution and start talking about how to support the people it’s leaving behind.

The robots aren’t just coming; they’re already here, and they’re taking our jobs.
