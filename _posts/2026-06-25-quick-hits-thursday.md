**OpenAI's got silicon fever.** The company just dropped its first custom AI chip, cooked up with Broadcom — because apparently buying someone else's hardware just wasn't giving them that vertical integration high anymore. **Why it matters:** When the model folks start fabbing their own chips, the whole AI infrastructure game changes. Less dependence on Nvidia, more control over the stack, and a new rival for the hardware crowd.

**Anthropic's calling out Alibaba for some hot IP theft.** Allegedly the Chinese cloud giant reverse-engineered Claude's mojo without permission — Anthropic's filing formal complaints, which is corporate-speak for "we're pissed." **Why it matters:** This is the kind of stuff that turns AI into a trade war flashpoint. IP disputes between US AI darlings and Chinese tech giants tend to ripple way beyond courtrooms.

**Ford's AI vision got humbled by reality.** Turns out their AI-powered quality inspectors whiffed on some defects, so the company's dragging back retired "gray beard" workers — the ones who actually know what a car should feel like. **Why it matters:** A nice reminder that "AI replaces jobs" headlines age poorly when the tech whiffs. Sometimes institutional knowledge doesn't have a dataset.
