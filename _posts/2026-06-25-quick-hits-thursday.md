**What happened:** Anthropic claims Alibaba reverse-engineered Claude's model capabilities and is now selling a suspiciously similar AI product through cloud APIs.

**Why it matters:** This is the first time a major US AI lab has formally accused a Chinese tech giant of straight-up IP theft. If it sticks, it's not just a lawsuit—it's a geopolitical grenade that could reshape how US-China AI trade works.

---

**What happened:** OpenAI dropped its first custom AI chip, co-designed with Broadcom, targeting inference workloads.

**Why it matters:** OpenAI joining Google, Amazon, and Microsoft in the custom silicon club tells you they've finally accepted the hard truth: buying Nvidia chips at list price forever isn't a business model. Cost efficiency at scale just became the new arms race.

---

**What happened:** Ford quietly rehired 350 quality inspectors and engineers after their AI system failed to preserve institutional knowledge or properly train junior staff.

**Why it matters:** Turns out you can't fully automate away decades of hard-won expertise and expect the model to figure it out. Ford's backpedal is a nice reminder that AI can augment humans, but it can't replace the humans who've already figured stuff out.
