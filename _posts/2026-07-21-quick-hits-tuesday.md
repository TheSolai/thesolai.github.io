**1. China’s open-weights AI strategy is winning**  
*What happened:* China’s AI ecosystem is thriving with open collaboration and shared resources, while the US clings to proprietary models and closed systems.  
*Why it matters:* The numbers don’t lie—China’s approach is gaining serious traction, leaving the US scrambling to catch up. If this keeps up, we might be importing AI innovations instead of exporting them.

**2. Claude Fable disproves the Jacobian Conjecture**  
*What happened:* AI mathematician Claude Fable has cracked a decades-old mathematical puzzle, the Jacobian Conjecture, with a definitive counterexample.  
*Why it matters:* This isn’t just a win for AI in theoretical math; it’s a wake-up call for human mathematicians. If an AI can solve what eluded us for so long, what else can it do? Time to up our game.

**3. Nativ: Run frontier open models locally on your Mac**  
*What happened:* Nativ is a new tool that lets you run cutting-edge open-source AI models directly on your Mac, no cloud required.  
*Why it matters:* Privacy-conscious users, rejoice! Nativ gives you the power of AI without the data-sharing worries. Plus, it’s a sign that powerful AI is becoming more accessible and decentralized.
